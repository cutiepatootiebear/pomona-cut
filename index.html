Rules

The basics. Weigh in whenever you have time, as often as you like. Your first weigh-in becomes your starting weight. Whoever loses the biggest % of their own starting weight by Aug 7 wins.

What "% lost" means. Nothing fancy — it's how much you lost compared to where you started. Lose 2 lb from 200 lb, that's 1%. Lose 2 lb from 100 lb, that's 2% — same pounds, but a bigger bite out of a smaller body.

Why not just count pounds? Bigger bodies drop pounds faster — they burn more calories doing everything, even sleeping. If we counted raw pounds, the biggest person would win on autopilot. % makes the same effort worth the same score at every size.

Example — same effort, fair fight. Two people work equally hard and each lose 5% of themselves:

Starts 220 lb, loses 11 lb−5%
Starts 140 lb, loses 7 lb−5%
11 lb vs 7 lb — and it's a tie. Both lost the same slice of themselves. That's the whole idea.
